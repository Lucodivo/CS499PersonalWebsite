How'd Houdini die?

A human by the name J. Gordon Whitehead asked Houdini "whether it was true that punches in the stomach did not hurt him." Whitehead suddenly proceeded to strike Houdini (who was reclined on a couch with a broken ankle) with four "hammer-like blows below the belt" until Houdini could gesture that he could take no more and that he did not expect Whitehead to so readily strike him. Houdini died shortly after as the result of appendicitis. His last words were: "I'm tired of fighting."
